In the nation with the world’s highest incarceration rate, a deep understanding of the factors that shape the U.S. as a prison society is essential for crafting an effective pastoral theological response. This paper explores how socioeconomic oppression—especially along lines of race and class—misconceptions about safety and justice, and psychosocial mechanisms such as othering and scapegoating contribute to mass incarceration. The author also proposes avenues for further theological reflection on the racist and imperial roots of the justice system.
