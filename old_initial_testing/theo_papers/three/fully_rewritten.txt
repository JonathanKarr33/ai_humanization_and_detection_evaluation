This essay offers theological reflection, theoretical frameworks, ethnographic insights, and pastoral proposals on religious environmentalism as a form of intersystemic care that links social and ecological justice. Focusing on the Environmental Guild of the Central Texas Episcopal Church, it illustrates how religious environmental praxis embodies ecologically inclusive relational justice—a key pastoral theological norm. The article examines theological and contextual rationales for pastoral engagement with ecological issues, arguing that such engagement provides a more comprehensive approach to addressing structural violence that undermines the flourishing of both humans and the wider ecological community. By integrating faith, daily practices, and care for creation, religious environmentalism offers pastoral theology a model for bridging social and ecological justice in both theory and practice.
