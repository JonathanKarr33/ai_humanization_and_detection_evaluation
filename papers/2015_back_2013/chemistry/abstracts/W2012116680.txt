Chemical models have been developed over the years by astrophysicists to\nstudy the pro- cesses at play in the various environments of the interstellar\nmedium (ISM) that define the chemical composition of the gas and the dust.\nThese qualitative aspects of the model predictions have been improved from a\nchemical point of view thanks to many recent developments of the experimental\ntechnics and theoretical methods that aim at studying the individual reactions\nin conditions as close to the ISM conditions as possible and characterize the\nrate constants of their efficiency. These models have also been more and more\nassociated with dynamical evolution of the ISM physical conditions (for star\nforming regions for instance) since the chemical composition is far from\nsteady-state in such regions. In this paper, we try to assess the state of the\nart concerning the chemical modeling of dark clouds, the initial step for the\nformation of stars and disks.\n
