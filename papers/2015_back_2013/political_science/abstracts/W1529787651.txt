This&#13;\n report aims to answer two main questions. First is have the&#13;\n SUS reforms transformed the health system as envisaged 20&#13;\n years ago? Second, have the reforms led to improvements with&#13;\n regard to access to services, financial protection, and&#13;\n health outcomes? In addressing these questions, the report&#13;\n revisits ground covered in previous assessments, but also&#13;\n brings to bear additional or more recent data and places&#13;\n Brazil's health system in an international context. The&#13;\n report shows that the health system reforms can be credited&#13;\n with significant achievements. The report points to some&#13;\n promising directions for health system reforms that will&#13;\n allow Brazil to continue building on the achievements made&#13;\n to date. Although it is possible to reach some broad&#13;\n conclusions, there are many gaps and caveats in the story. A&#13;\n secondary aim of the report is to consider how some of these&#13;\n gaps can be filled through improved monitoring of health&#13;\n system performance and future research. The introduction&#13;\n presents a short review of the history of the SUS, describes&#13;\n the core principles that underpinned the reform, and offers&#13;\n a brief description of the evaluation framework used in the&#13;\n report. Chapter two presents findings on the extent to which&#13;\n the SUS reforms have transformed the health system, focusing&#13;\n on delivery, financing, and governance. Chapter three asks&#13;\n whether the reforms have resulted in improved outcomes with&#13;\n regard to access to services, financial protection, quality,&#13;\n health outcomes, and efficiency.
