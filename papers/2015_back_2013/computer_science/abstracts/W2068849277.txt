As we are moving towards the Internet of Things (IoT), the number of sensors\ndeployed around the world is growing at a rapid pace. Market research has shown\na significant growth of sensor deployments over the past decade and has\npredicted a significant increment of the growth rate in the future. These\nsensors continuously generate enormous amounts of data. However, in order to\nadd value to raw sensor data we need to understand it. Collection, modelling,\nreasoning, and distribution of context in relation to sensor data plays\ncritical role in this challenge. Context-aware computing has proven to be\nsuccessful in understanding sensor data. In this paper, we survey context\nawareness from an IoT perspective. We present the necessary background by\nintroducing the IoT paradigm and context-aware fundamentals at the beginning.\nThen we provide an in-depth analysis of context life cycle. We evaluate a\nsubset of projects (50) which represent the majority of research and commercial\nsolutions proposed in the field of context-aware computing conducted over the\nlast decade (2001-2011) based on our own taxonomy. Finally, based on our\nevaluation, we highlight the lessons to be learnt from the past and some\npossible directions for future research. The survey addresses a broad range of\ntechniques, methods, models, functionalities, systems, applications, and\nmiddleware solutions related to context awareness and IoT. Our goal is not only\nto analyse, compare and consolidate past research work but also to appreciate\ntheir findings and discuss their applicability towards the IoT.\n
