This proposal details the Segregated and Normative AGI Architecture Proposal (SAAP), a design framework based on strict functional segregation to mitigate existential risk and value drift. Unlike monolithic architectures that treat safety as an optimization target, SAAP enforces a control hierarchy where operational efficiency is architecturally subordinate to inmutable security. By formalizing Deontological Axiomatic Constraints (CAD) through an extreme dual loss function and mandating a transparent stop-and-delegate protocol during critical decision-making, SAAP establishes the minimum design criteria required for robustness. A key contribution is the quantification of the structural cost a projected 50-70% throughput reduction in high-risk scenarios positioning this inefficiency not as a flaw, but as the necessary price for auditable human sovereignty in AGI governance.
