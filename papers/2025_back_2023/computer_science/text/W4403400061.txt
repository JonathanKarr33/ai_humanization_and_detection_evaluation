Preface
Vehicle system dynamics is an emerging discipline that has developed in modern
times, encompassing all the sciences related to automotive motion, with rich and
extensive content. Speciﬁcally, vehicle system dynamics studies the mechanisms of
longitudinal, lateral, and vertical motion of vehicles, as well as the dynamic charac-
teristics and control technologies of various subsystems and assemblies of vehicles
derived from these three movements.
With the rapid development of automotive electronic control technology, people
are more and more aware of the importance of vehicle system dynamics. The current
advanced active safety systems in automobiles, such as anti-lock braking system
(ABS), electronic stability program (ESP), adaptive cruise control (ACC), accelera-
tion slip regulation (ASR), electronic brake force distribution (EBD), etc., are based
on vehicle dynamics as a theoretical basis for research and development and design.
Therefore, for the undergraduate and graduate students majoring in vehicle engi-
neering and the related engineering technicians who will be engaged in the research
and development of vehicle engineering technology in the future, it is necessary to
study the vehicle system dynamics systematically and deeply.
With the development of modern vehicle performance and speed, and the applica-
tion of chassis control technology, the research content of vehicle system dynamics
is more and more abundant. In the process of writing this book, a large amount of
research and material collection are conducted to track the latest industry devel-
opment status. Combined with the author’s years of teaching and research experi-
ence in the industry, the latest vehicle handling inverse dynamics, non-pneumatic
tire dynamics, vehicle state and parameter estimation methods, and driver-vehicle
closed-loop control system dynamics methods are taken as the key chapters of this
book. It fully reﬂects the current advanced technology status and development trend
of vehicle system dynamics. In addition, this book systematically combs and classi-
ﬁes the knowledge points of vehicle system dynamics, forming a highly systematic
framework structure, aiming to help readers deeply understand the knowledge points
of vehicle system dynamics.

Introduction
This book covers the basic theoretical systems of vehicle system dynamics, based
on the modeling and theoretical methods of longitudinal, lateral, and vertical system
dynamics. The research results of pneumatic/non-pneumatic tire dynamics, vehicle
handling inverse dynamics, vehicle state and parameter estimation method, new
evaluation method of driver-vehicle closed-loop system handling dynamics, four-
wheel steering vehicle motion stability and vehicle suspension control, and so on are
introduced in detail. In the process of reading this book, readers will not only have a
comprehensive understanding of the framework system of vehicle system dynamics,
but also have a deeper grasp of the connotation and denotation of the discipline.
This bookcanbeusedas acoursematerial for undergraduateandgraduatestudents
in the ﬁeld of vehicle engineering. In addition, this book for the vehicle professional
teaching and research personnel also has a good reference value.
