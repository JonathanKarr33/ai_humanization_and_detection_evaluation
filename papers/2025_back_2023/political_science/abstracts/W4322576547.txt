Educational inclusion policies do not always have their intended impact and have paradoxical consequences, and this risk of potentially counter productive or exclusionary effects can be heightened when policies are informed by politicised visions of social inclusion. Focusing on the Spanish context, this chapter argues that a rethink of the inclusion model that, to some extent, is already present in regional schools and training centres is timely. This chapter reports a thematic review of selected published studies and policy reviews and considers both historical and current policy initiatives and their effects. It highlights issues such as responsibility for the development of inclusion-related programmes in Spain and their implementation.
