For centuries England and Russia most often acted as antagonists during&#x0D; major military conflicts. Only three times in the century-long history of relations were&#x0D; they military-political allies and partners: in the late 18th and early 19th centuries&#x0D; during the First and Second World Wars. In this article, based on the analysis of the&#x0D; «Journal of the Dutch Expedition» by Ivan Fiodorov – a document stored in the archives of the Federal Institute of the Russian History of the Russian Academy of&#x0D; Sciences of St. Petersburg, which had never before been involved by historians, an attempt is made to characterize (based on the text of this document) Anglo-Russian military cooperation in the years of the Second Coalition war against Republican France, an important element of which was so-called «Secret Expedition» in the autumn of 1799 to Holland in which both English and Russian troops took part.
