10 A many times heard mantra of solvent degradation management in amine-based post combustion capture is “keep the solvent 11 clean” to minimize solvent consumption. It is assumed that the amine losses would decrease by the removal of metals, 12 degradation products, and reactive trace components which are captured from the flue gas, like NO2 (as potentially driving 13 components of the amine degradation besides dissolved O2). However, this theoretical hypothesis – based on results from 14 laboratory experiments typically generated with fresh amines – disregards the complexity of the solvent matrix, interaction of 15 potential metal catalysts with degradation products and oxidizing agents, and specific chemical requirements which must be 16 fulfilled before a degradation mechanism can proceed.
