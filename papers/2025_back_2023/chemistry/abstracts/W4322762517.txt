A series of novel surface Ru-H bipyridine complexes-grafted TiO<sub>2</sub> nanohybrids were for the first time prepared by a combined procedure of surface organometallic chemistry with post-synthetic ligand exchange for photocatalytic conversion of CO<sub>2</sub> to CH<sub>4</sub> with H<sub>2</sub> as electron and proton donors under visible light irradiation. The selectivity toward CH<sub>4</sub> increased to 93.4% by the ligand exchange of 4,4'-dimethyl-2,2'-bipyridine (4,4'-bpy) with the surface cyclopentadienyl (Cp)-RuH complex and the CO<sub>2</sub> methanation activity was enhanced by 4.4-fold. An impressive rate of 241.2 μL·g<sup>-1</sup>·h<sup>-1</sup> for CH<sub>4</sub> production was achieved over the optimal photocatalyst. The femtosecond transient IR absorption results demonstrated that the hot electrons were fast injected in 0.9 ps from the photoexcited surface 4,4'-bpy-RuH complex into the conduction band of TiO<sub>2</sub> nanoparticles to form a charge-separated state with an average lifetime of ca. 50.0 ns responsible for the CO<sub>2</sub> methanation. The spectral characterizations indicated clearly that the formation of CO<sub>2</sub><sup>•-</sup> radicals by single electron reduction of CO<sub>2</sub> molecules adsorbed on surface oxygen vacancies of TiO<sub>2</sub> nanoparticles was the most critical step for the methanation. Such radical intermediates were inserted into the explored Ru-H bond to generate Ru-OOCH species and finally CH<sub>4</sub> and H<sub>2</sub>O in the presence of H<sub>2</sub>.
