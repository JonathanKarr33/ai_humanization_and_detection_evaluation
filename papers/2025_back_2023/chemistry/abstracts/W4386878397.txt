The electrocatalytic nitrate (NO<sub>3</sub><sup>-</sup>) reduction reaction (eNITRR) is a promising method for ammonia synthesis. However, its efficacy is currently limited due to poor selectivity, largely caused by the inherent complexity of the multiple-electron processes involved. To address these issues, oxygen-vacancy-rich LaFe<sub>0.9</sub>M<sub>0.1</sub>O<sub>3-δ</sub> (M = Co, Ni, and Cu) perovskite submicrofibers have been designed from the starting material LaFeO<sub>3-δ</sub> (LF) by a B-site substitution strategy and used as the eNITRR electrocatalyst. Consequently, the LaFe<sub>0.9</sub>Cu<sub>0.1</sub>O<sub>3-δ</sub> (LF<sub>0.9</sub>Cu<sub>0.1</sub>) submicrofibers with a stronger Fe-O hybridization, more oxygen vacancies, and more positive surface potential exhibit a higher ammonia yield rate of 349 ± 15 μg h<sup>-1</sup> mg<sup>-1</sup><sub>cat.</sub> and a Faradaic efficiency of 48 ± 2% than LF submicrofibers. The <i>COMSOL</i> Multiphysics simulations demonstrate that the more positive surface of LF<sub>0.9</sub>Cu<sub>0.1</sub> submicrofibers can induce NO<sub>3</sub><sup>-</sup> enrichment and suppress the competing hydrogen evolution reaction. By combining a variety of <i>in situ</i> characterizations and density functional theory calculations, the eNITRR mechanism is revealed, where the first proton-electron coupling step (*NO<sub>3</sub> + H<sup>+</sup> + e<sup>-</sup> → *HNO<sub>3</sub>) is the rate-determining step with a reduced energy barrier of 1.83 eV. This work highlights the positive effect of cation substitution in promoting eNITRR properties of perovskites and provides new insights into the studies of perovskite-type electrocatalytic ammonia synthesis catalysts.
