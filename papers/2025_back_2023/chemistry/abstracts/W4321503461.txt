Atom trapping leads to catalysts with atomically dispersed Ru<sub>1</sub>O<sub>5</sub> sites on (100) facets of ceria, as identified by spectroscopy and DFT calculations. This is a new class of ceria-based materials with Ru properties drastically different from the known M/ceria materials. They show excellent activity in catalytic NO oxidation, a critical step that requires use of large loadings of expensive noble metals in diesel aftertreatment systems. Ru<sub>1</sub>/CeO<sub>2</sub> is stable during continuous cycling, ramping, and cooling as well as the presence of moisture. Furthermore, Ru<sub>1</sub>/CeO<sub>2</sub> shows very high NO<sub><i>x</i></sub> storage properties due to formation of stable Ru-NO complexes as well as a high spill-over rate of NO<sub><i>x</i></sub> onto CeO<sub>2</sub>. Only ∼0.05 wt % of Ru is required for excellent NO<sub><i>x</i></sub> storage. Ru<sub>1</sub>O<sub>5</sub> sites exhibit much higher stability during calcination in air/steam up to 750 °C in contrast to RuO<sub>2</sub> nanoparticles. We clarify the location of Ru(II) ions on the ceria surface and experimentally identify the mechanism of NO storage and oxidation using DFT calculations and in situ DRIFTS/mass spectroscopy. Moreover, we show excellent reactivity of Ru<sub>1</sub>/CeO<sub>2</sub> for NO reduction by CO at low temperatures: only 0.1-0.5 wt % of Ru is sufficient to achieve high activity. Modulation-excitation in situ infrared and XPS measurements reveal the individual elementary steps of NO reduction by CO on an atomically dispersed Ru ceria catalyst, highlighting unique properties of Ru<sub>1</sub>/CeO<sub>2</sub> and its propensity to form oxygen vacancies/Ce<sup>+3</sup> sites that are critical for NO reduction, even at low Ru loadings. Our study highlights the applicability of novel ceria-based single-atom catalysts to NO and CO abatement.
